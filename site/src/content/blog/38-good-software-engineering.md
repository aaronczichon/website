> Disclaimer: All I wrote here is my own opinion and not related to any company I work for or worked for.
> I also know that everyone has an opinion on AI and nearly everything has said in that occasion but nonetheless I think that I need to write down my 5 cents on that. It probably is only for my future self but public, so ¯\_(ツ)_/¯

I‘m a software engineer now for more than 13 years and I love it. And now some people are saying that my profession is no longer relevant and software will be a disposable product.

AI is currently changing industries. Yes it‘s also coming to other industries (mostly forced by some management decision with or without purport) but where you feel the change the most is the software industry.
We all heard the talks about „software is dead“, „everybody can code now“, „we don‘t need software engineers anymore“, „I‘m a one man company with agents now“, „software is disposable now!“, „we all gonna loose our work“ and so on.
I would mostly disagree with these statements. Although they can be true in very, very specific scenarios.

For example let‘s pick the statement „AI will cut employment rates and many people will get unemployed“.
This may be true for some professions and in the short term. We currently see, especially the larger companies, firing engineers for the sake of AI. But that‘s only the short term view on the change.
First: The whole industry was over employed over the last years. Companies hired IT people just for the sake of hiring IT people. Talent crawling was a thing. There was not enough work for everyone inside many companies to work on something. Many engineers, especially on larger „digital hubs“ like the Valley, Berlin, London, etc. where hired and wheren‘t force to delivery real results. Often just that another company is not hiring them.
Now the smaller tasks which they had and which where not critical are delegated to AI (I‘m not here to judge if this is good or not).
Second: Industries are always changing. I don‘t want to draw similarities between the industrialization und now. This time let‘s pick agriculture for comparison.
In agriculture people also thought that they will be useless with the use of horses and later with machines. But that‘s not true at all. The work itself has changed. Now you no longer need people working the fields. You need people building tractors, drones and more to help others managing their fields and agricultural landscape.
And I think this is something people oversee for the sake of AI. Yes, some things are changing but I don‘t think there will be less work. It will be other work. But there still will be work.

Let‘s pick another thing I currently hear often: Software is a disposable product because everyone can create it now.
I strongly disagree with this statement. I want to draw another comparison here for better understanding.
Something I read made this very clear: `Bread is something everyone can bake easily at home, yet 99% of it is brought`.*
This is a good analogy to the current, and maybe future, state of the software industry. Yes, everybody can now create a small app. Maybe even large apps. But 99% of the people will not do it. The same people that don‘t bake their bread at home.
Yes, AI software can be good enough, but if you want good and high quality software you will still buy it from the people (and companies) that are good at it.

Some may now scream: „Yes, that‘s why I don‘t use AI!“. But this is not what I‘m trying to say. People working in a bakery don‘t use the same techniques like 300 years ago, not even the same like 20 years ago. They don‘t even do everything on their own. Some get pre-made dough from other companies. People still buy their goods at these bakeries.
Good software engineers get better with AI as another tool in their toolbox. I like writing software development. I like creating the concepts of a software solution. But maybe I don‘t like writing every boilerplate code, developing every routine if a deadline is near or writing every test which is similar to the others before?

We still need to learn a lot on how to use AI properly and when **not** to use it.
I‘m excited what the next couple months and years will bring in terms of local AI models. I also look quite interested towards the developments of the current pricing strategies. I think it‘s obvious that the current money-burning strategies of the large AI companies won‘t work forever. We already see price increases across the board. Especially with the latest models.
Like everything in tech: We have something quite new, we don‘t know (yet) how to use it correctly (if there is such) and everything is developing really fast compared to other industries (this time even fast for us in tech).
